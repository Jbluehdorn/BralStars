# Air Envelopes & Gravity in Wildspace
Wildspace has its own physics rules distinct from standard D&D.

## Air Envelopes
Every object of Tiny size or larger in Wildspace is surrounded by an **air envelope** — a bubble of breathable air that extends roughly as far from the object as the object's longest dimension.

- A human has about a 1-foot air envelope
- A spelljamming ship has an envelope extending to its hull edges (and a bit beyond)
- Envelopes merge when objects get close — two ships side by side share air

### Air Quality
Air in an envelope degrades over time if not refreshed:
| Duration | Quality | Effect |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Fresh | < 24 hrs old | No effect |
| Fouled | 24–72 hrs | Disadvantage on Constitution saves |
| Deadly | 72+ hrs | Poison damage each hour |

Plants, certain spells, and ship alchemical systems can refresh air.

## Gravity
- Every object of Large size or larger in Wildspace generates a **gravity plane** — a flat horizontal field through the widest part of the object
- Objects on either side of the plane fall **toward** it (effective gravity = roughly 1g)
- On a ship, the gravity plane runs through the middle of the hull — crew on top fall toward the deck; crew on the bottom (the keel) also fall toward the deck
- In open Wildspace with no large objects nearby, there is **no gravity** — creatures and objects drift freely

### Gravity and Combat
- Creatures knocked off a ship into open space drift away — no falling damage, but they're stranded
- Moving in zero-g requires a surface to push off or a successful Athletics check (DC 10)
