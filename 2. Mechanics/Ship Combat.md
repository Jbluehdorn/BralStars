# Ship Combat
Spelljammer ship combat plays out in **combat rounds** like regular D&D, but with additional considerations for positioning, crew actions, and ship damage.

## Initiative
- Ships act on the **pilot's initiative**
- The pilot uses their action to maneuver the ship each round
- All other crew members act on their own initiative

## Crew Stations
| Station | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Pilot | Maneuver the ship (uses pilot's action) |
| Gunner | Fire a weapon (uses gunner's action) |
| Boarding crew | Attack enemies directly |
| Damage control | Repair hull damage (Medicine or Tinker's Tools DC 15) |

## Ship Actions (Pilot)
- **Ram** — move into an enemy ship's space; both ships take damage based on size difference
- **Turn** — change heading up to 90 degrees
- **Full Speed** — move up to the ship's full speed rating
- **Evasive Maneuvers** — disadvantage on attacks against the ship until next turn; ship moves at half speed

## Ship Damage
- Ships have **hit points** and an **AC** (based on construction)
- At **0 HP**, the ship is disabled (drifting, no propulsion)
- Critical hits against a ship can disable weapon mounts, breach the hull, or kill crew

## Boarding
- Crew can leap between ships when within 5 feet
- Grappling hooks and ropes can be used to lash ships together

## Morale
When a ship loses more than half its HP or its captain is killed, the DM may call for a **Morale check** (Wisdom DC 12) for the opposing crew — failure means they attempt to flee or surrender.
