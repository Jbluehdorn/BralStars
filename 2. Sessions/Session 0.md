# Session 0 — Run Sheet

> Format: DM-facing notes. Talk through each section in order. Keep it conversational — this is a collaborative session, not a lecture.

---

## 1. West Marches Format (talk-through)

- **Players drive scheduling.** You post in the group chat when you want to play. I don't schedule sessions.
- **No fixed party.** Any combination of PCs can go on any adventure. Different crews can be out at the same time (in fiction).
- **Jobs & rumors** live on the **Adventurers' Board** in [[The Laughing Beholder]]. Pick a hook, gather a crew, tell me the goal.
- **Home base is the Rock.** Adventures go *out* and come *back*. Downtime happens on the Rock.
- **Recaps required.** Whoever wants XP/loot logged posts a short recap after the session.
- I'm borrowing the *structure*, not every rule — we'll bend things as needed.

---

## 2. Emergent Gameplay (talk-through)

- I'm not writing a plot. I'm prepping **situations** — factions, NPCs, locations, and conflicts that all *want things*.
- **The story comes from what you do**, not from a script. If you ignore a thread, it keeps moving without you. If you push on one, it reacts.
- No railroad, no "right" answer. Consequences are real; the world remembers.
- Your job as players: **have goals, take action, follow curiosity.** Passive PCs get passive stories.
- My job: play the world honestly and let the pieces collide.

---

## 5. Character Creation

Standard chargen (race, class, background, etc.) — but every PC needs **two things** before play:

### 5a. External Goal
- **Achievable. Measurable. Concrete.**
- Something the character is actively trying to *do* in the world.
- Good: "Buy back my family's ship from the Wreckwrights." / "Kill the pirate captain who marooned me." / "Get elected to the Merchants' Council."
- Bad: "Become rich." / "Be a hero." / "Find adventure." (Too vague — no finish line.)
- **Test:** Can we tell when it's done? If not, sharpen it.

### 5b. Internal Conflict
- A **flaw, contradiction, or wound** the character carries.
- Not a quirk — a real tension that will get in the way.
- Good: "I trust no one, but I'm desperately lonely." / "I believe in the law, but my brother is a smuggler." / "I'm a coward pretending to be brave."
- Bad: "I like ale." / "I'm grumpy."
- **Test:** Will this cause hard choices? If not, dig deeper.
