# Scene 1 — The Laughing Beholder

**Setting:** [[The Laughing Beholder]], open mic night. Warm, loud, half-drunk.

## The Table
- **[[Clara Cornhole]]** (halfling barmaid) herds the party to a front table. Drops a pitcher, vanishes.
  - *"Move it, move it, table's held, don't spill on me, sit down."*
- **[[Swangford P. McAndress]]** (timid drow) is whisper-singing on stage. Crowd is cruel. If a PC helps him, he becomes a recurring NPC who *remembers*.

## Large Luigi's Pitch
Beholder drifts over the table. Broken Common, booming voice.
- *"WELCOME! DRINK IS ON HOUSE! WELL — FIRST ONE!"*
- *"LARGE RATS IN BASEMENT! TAKE FROM CUSTOMERS! YOU GO FIND AND SMASH!"*
- *"BIG RATS. NOT NORMAL. TAKE SHINY. TAKE GOLD."*
- *"YOU SMASH, YOU GET PAID. YOU DIE — I KEEP GOLD."*

**If asked:**
- How many? *"MANY!"*
- How big? *"THIS BIG."* (Spreads eyestalks a foot apart.)
- Anything weird? Pause. *"...THEY TAKE SHINY THING. RATS DON'T DO THAT."*

**DM secrets:** Luigi's broken Common is a choice — he understands everything perfectly. Won't negotiate gold; will throw in free meals.

## The Snatch
- Cat-sized rat darts from under a nearby table.
- Beelines for the flashiest PC. **Sleight of Hand vs passive Perception** — weight the odds, the rat wins.
- Rat pauses, stands on hind legs, holds the item like a person, makes **eye contact**, tips a not-quite-hat, bolts to the kitchen stairs.
- **Luigi, without looking:** *"...SEE? THAT ONE! GO! SMASH!"*

**Do not roll for the chase.** The rat gets away. If a PC casts in the tavern: *"NO MAGIC IN COMMON ROOM! DOWNSTAIRS!"*

# Scene 2 — The Basement

Storeroom under the tavern. Kegs, crates, cured meats, jars of pickled unknowns.

**Three things to find:**

### 1. The Crack in the Wall
- Between kegs, far end. 8-inch gap, angled down.
- Greasy smudges, tiny bootprints.
- Small squeezes through. Medium needs help (Str check, teamwork).

### 2. [[Charlee Chees]]
- Mouse on a barrel. Purple shirt, jean shorts, fly shoes, wire glasses. Talks like a 90s Nickelodeon skater.
- *"Yo. 'Bout time somebody with legs showed up."*
- **What he offers:**
  - Rats are sapient, wear stuff, run a crew.
  - His brother **[[Chester Chees|Chester]]** got grabbed a few days back. Held in the hideout.
  - Will guide the party if they promise to save Chester.
- Mice and rats are separate factions. Rats bully mice.

### 3. Luigi's Ledger
- On a table in the middle of the room, in plain sight (Luigi's arrogance is the security).
- Investigation reveals: **Luigi is cheating on his taxes.** Two sets of books, named contacts.
- **Leverage on a beholder.** Options: leave it, take it, report it, tell Luigi. Any choice reshapes the relationship.

## Scene End — The Shrinking
Once the party finds the crack:
- Luigi's eyestalk peeks around the doorway (nobody notices).
- Drifts down silently. **Eight eye rays lock on. No save.**
- Party is now ~6 inches tall. Gear scales with them. Coin does not.
- Charlee, unaffected: *"...whoa."*
- Luigi: ***"OK! GOOD LUCK! GO GET RATS NOW!"*** Floats back upstairs.

**Effects at rat-scale:**
- The crack is now a cavern mouth.
- Dropped nail = spear. A cat would be a TPK.
- Duration: until Luigi decides (i.e. until the job's done).
- If a player protests: *"IS TEMPORARY! PROBABLY!"*

# Scene 3 — The Drainage Tunnel *(optional, cut for time)*

**Cut-guide:** Skip if session is running long — funnel straight to hideout. Otherwise: establishes insect faction, lets Charlee shine.

## Setup
- Pre-Bral tunnel: purple-black stone, organic curves, faint humming (Crucible foreshadow).
- Opens onto a ledge over a **rat-scale rushing river of garbage** — 20 ft across, fast, loud. Trash-islands.

## The Crossing (soft puzzle)
- Hop islands: **DC 13 Dex** on impact or it breaks loose.
- Improvised raft (splinter of wood), rope, spells, ride the current.
- No skill challenge — call rolls only when a plan hits real risk.

## The Cockroaches
Mid-crossing, **[[Manny]]** and **[[Ramirez]]** drop in — party-sized, LA Cholo, arms crossed (all four).
- *"Órale, órale, órale. Where you think you goin', ese?"*
- Suspicious, not hostile. Think the party's here to rob the pantry.
- **Magic word: Charlee.** Name-drop him or let him step up → they soften and help.
- If it goes hostile: use *stirge* or *fire beetle* stats, drop damage, emphasize speed + flight. Retreat when bloodied. Killing them poisons the insect faction.

## Onward
Past the far bank: cigarette-butt torches, tiny graffiti, rat-scale "NO TRESPASSING" scratched into stone. Hideout is close.

# Scene 4 — The Rat Hideout

> Hideout is large; map fills in the rest. These are the beats that matter.

## Beat 1 — Front Door & [[Tommy Lock-Lips Daluca|Tommy "Lock-Lips" Daluca]]
- Reinforced rat-scale door, locked. Tommy snoring in a chair with a **tommy gun** on his lap.
- **Standing orders:** Di Frommagio family only.

**Options:**
- **Sneak past** — Stealth vs his poor passive Perception. Generous unless someone clangs armor.
- **Loot him (asleep):** coat pocket has a scrap reading **"BUT"**. Also lint, cheese rind, half a cigarette.
- **Take him out quietly.**
- **Wake and bluff:** hard, grumpy when woken.

### The Password Puzzle
- Base word: **CHEESE**. Rotates daily via **Vigenère cipher** with the day's key.
- **Today's key:** `BUT`
- **Today's spoken password:** `DBXFMX`

| CHEESE | + BUT | = |
|---|---|---|
| C | B | **D** |
| H | U | **B** |
| E | T | **X** |
| E | B | **F** |
| S | U | **M** |
| E | T | **X** |

- Reward solving it (deeper rats greet them as family). Don't punish skipping it — fight/sneak/bluff work too.

## Beat 2 — The Nest (cells, western wall)
Sign: **THE NEST**. Cellblock, guard table, half-eaten cheese wedge.

### Guards (backs to door, distracted by dice — advantage on stealth)
- **[[Jimmy The Tail Branzino|Jimmy "The Tail" Branzino]]** — on-duty guard. Has cell keys on his belt.
- **[[Bobby Malone]]** — freeloading buddy, not supposed to be here.

### Prisoner — [[Chester Chees]]
- Orange-spotted mouse, glasses, fast-talker. Lights up on seeing party (especially Charlee).
- **Knows:**
  - Awaiting [[Don Leto di Frommagio]]'s judgment. Don is deeper in.
  - Vault room further in holds all the stolen shinies.
  - "The room that hums" — rats avoid but revere it (the Crucible).
  - Jimmy has the keys.

**Options:** ambush · pickpocket keys (Sleight of Hand) · bluff with `DBXFMX` · pick the lock (DC 12) · distract-and-extract.

**Payoff:** Charlee's loyalty. Chester spills full hideout intel on the way back. Will fight if pressed (badly).

## Beat 3 — The Slop (dining hall, center)
Sign: **SLOP**. Long table, five rats hunched over meat-and-cheese soup. Weapons within reach but nobody's looking up. Kitchen north through a swinging door.

### Diners
- **[[Billy Skeeter]]** & **[[Joey Skeeter]]** — bickering brothers. Fight back-to-back (advantage while adjacent).
- **[[Johnny Jerk-Off Ramone|Johnny "Jerk-Off" Ramone]]**
- **[[Slimey Rizzo]]** — always damp.
- **[[Skinny Pete]]** — eats twice his weight.

**Options:**
- **Walk through** with the password — nod and "hey", nobody looks up.
- **Sneak** — easy while eating; hard once a fight breaks out elsewhere.
- **Combat** — 5 rats + kitchen. Scalding soup is an improvised weapon either way.
- **Sabotage the stockpot** — takes the whole room out, with moral weight.

### Kitchen (north)
- Bathtub-sized stockpot. Rat-scale cleavers = daggers to party.
- 1–2 non-combatant cooks. They scream, which brings Slop.
- Side door / dumbwaiter / vent = quiet bypass of Slop entirely.

## Beat 4 — The Unknown Device *(drop when it feels right)*

Brass-and-obsidian, lunchbox-sized, warm to the touch. Not rat-made. Older. Weirder.

**Parts:**
1. Top ring — A–Z, rotates (plaintext).
2. Second ring — A–Z, rotates (key).
3. Button.
4. Milky crystal screen.

**How it works:** Set plaintext letter, set key letter, press button, screen flashes cipher letter. It's a Vigenère machine — party doesn't need the word, just the pattern.

**Bridge to the vault:** CHEESE + BUT → `DBXFMX`. Reveal one letter at a time; let players write it down.

- Portable if the party thinks to grab it. Origin ambiguous on purpose (possible illithid tech).

## Beat 5 — East Hall (sleeping quarters & vault)

| Door | Room |
|---|---|
| North | Empty sleeping quarters |
| Middle | Occupied — Pauley & Mary Anne |
| South | Loot Storeroom (password `DBXFMX`) |

### Room 1 — Empty Quarters
- Rat bunks, matchbox dressers, playing cards.
- **Loot:** pocket change, flask, half a love letter, spare pistol under a pillow.
- Good ambush / fallback spot.

### Room 2 — Pauley & Mary Anne
- Candle stub, tiny wine bottle, petals. Rat-scale **sock on the doorknob**.
- **[[Pauley Love Boy Amaretto|Pauley "Love Boy" Amaretto]]** — young made man, off-duty, wildly uninterested in being interrupted.
- **[[Mary Anne Susan Calivari di Frommagio]]** — his girlfriend. Giggles. Has a hairpin she'd stab with.
- **She is the Don's daughter.** They don't lead with it — but it's *the* fact that makes this room dangerous.
- **Secret:** they want *out* of the life. Can't run — Don Leto would hunt Pauley to the ends of Wildspace. A perceptive party could offer them a way out (favor / lifelong debt).

**Reactions:**
- Barge in → Pauley grabs a weapon, Mary Anne shrieks.
- Knock with `DBXFMX` → *"go 'way, we're busy!"* Can be talked past.
- Stealth → muffled giggling. Move on.
- **Not looking for a fight.** Surrender or flee if pressed.
- **If Mary Anne is harmed → blood feud.** Campaign-level consequence.
- **If Mary Anne is taken as leverage → Don negotiates anything.**

### Room 3 — The Loot Storeroom (vault)
Heavy reinforced door with a wheel-dial lock. Above it, scratched in crude rat-hand:

> **"SAY CHEESE"**

The password. In plain text. Above the door. The rats have not thought this through.

- **Password:** speak `CHEESE` (or dial `DBXFMX`) → click.
- **Alt entry:** DC 18 Thieves' Tools. Force is loud, brings the hideout down.

**Inside:**
- **Stolen shinies:** ~300 gp mixed coin (heavy at rat-scale), 6–8 pieces of jewelry (25–100 gp each), a **signet ring** (TBD after Session 0), a locket with a portrait, a golden thimble, a bone die.
- **Crate of tommy guns** — rat-scale = perfect for the party. Enough for everyone.
- Optional: rat-version ledger cataloging stashed jewelry by neighborhood.

**Tommy guns:**
- Treat as muskets or pistols. Noisy, brutal at rat-scale.
- When Luigi un-shrinks the party, guns shrink back in their hands. Keepable curios, not campaign artillery.

## Beat 6 — Don Leto's Office (the Crucible Chamber)

Deepest room. Carpeted, matchbox-wood panels, portrait of a large rat, crackling fireplace at the back wall, desk of stacked dominoes.

Behind the desk, floor to ceiling: **the [[Illithid Crucible]].** Giant purple gem, alien metal base, dead tentacles that once interfaced with illithid machinery. Hums at the edge of hearing. *Awesome* at rat-scale. This is what made the rats sapient.

### In the room
- **[[Don Leto di Frommagio]]** — behind the desk. Calm. Listens more than he speaks.
- **[[Donny Knuckles Moreno|Donny "Knuckles" Moreno]]** — bruiser, right shoulder. Cracking knuckles.
- **[[Timothee Chalamet]]** — skinny spellcaster menace, left shoulder. Fingers idle in the air.

Mid-conversation: **the next big heist** + **the gang's future.** Great overheard-dialogue moment — salt with Session 2 hooks (Crucible, uptown target, rival family).

### Options
- **Overhear from cover** — free intel.
- **Negotiate** — Don will *talk*. Proud, dangerous, reasonable. Leverage helps (Chester alive, Mary Anne unharmed, Crucible knowledge).
- **Combat:**
  - **Donny & Timothee fight to the death.** No surrender.
  - **Don Leto fights valiantly** until true mortal danger, then **escapes through the secret tunnel at the back of the fireplace.** Back wall of the hearth swings inward on a hinge.
  - Find the tunnel after: DC 15 Investigation, or shove the fireplace apart.

### Loot
- **Crucible** — immovable. Touching it: **DC 14 Wis save or 2d6 psychic** + glimpse a memory that isn't yours.
- **Don's desk** — family ledger, strongbox (~500 gp in gems), correspondence with an uptown name the party won't recognize yet.
- **Fireplace tunnel** — leads up into an unexplored part of the Rock.

**DM:** Don escaping is the intended out. Killing him deletes a great long-term villain — only let it happen if the party earns it.
