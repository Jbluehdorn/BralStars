# Character Creation

Use these guidelines when building your character for **Bral Stars**.

---

## Sources Allowed

- **Player's Handbook** — all races, classes, backgrounds
- **Spelljammer: Adventures in Space** — strongly recommended for race options (Astral Elves, Autognomes, Giff, Hadozee, Plasmoids, Thri-kreen)
- **Other official sourcebooks** — ask the DM first

---

## Your Hook

Every character needs a reason to be on the Rock. Pick one or invent your own:

- You arrived on a ship and never left
- You're a native — born and raised in Low City (or Middle, or High City)
- You're running from something on your home world
- You came looking for someone
- You came for the money
- You came because a map led you here
- You were press-ganged onto a ship and the Rock was the first port

---

## External Goal + Internal Conflict

Every PC needs **two things** before play:

### External Goal
**Achievable. Measurable. Concrete.** Something the character is actively trying to *do* in the world.
- Good: "Buy back my family's ship from the Wreckwrights." · "Kill the pirate captain who marooned me." · "Get elected to the Merchants' Council."
- Bad: "Become rich." · "Be a hero." · "Find adventure." *(too vague — no finish line)*
- **Test:** Can we tell when it's done? If not, sharpen it.

### Internal Conflict
A **flaw, contradiction, or wound** the character carries. Not a quirk — a real tension that will get in the way.
- Good: "I trust no one, but I'm desperately lonely." · "I believe in the law, but my brother is a smuggler." · "I'm a coward pretending to be brave."
- Bad: "I like ale." · "I'm grumpy."
- **Test:** Will this cause hard choices? If not, dig deeper.

---

## Connections

Every character needs **a reason to go on adventures** — something that pulls them off the Rock and into danger.

Good reasons: debt, curiosity, greed, a missing person, a reputation to build, a score to settle, wanderlust, a ship that needs a crew.

Additionally, pick **one faction connection** from the [[Factions & Connections]] page — an ally, a rival, a debt, or a history.

> [!warning] One rule
> Your character can't be so tied down that they can't leave. A guildmaster, a city official, a shopkeeper with daily obligations — these characters can't realistically drop everything for a three-hour adventure. Make sure your concept has the **freedom to go**.

---

## Starting Equipment

Standard class starting equipment from the PHB. Additionally, every character starts with:

- Lodgings on the Rock (Low City bunk, shared room, or better if your background supports it)
- A reason to check the [[Adventurers' Board]]
- One rumor about the Rock that may or may not be true

---

## Leveling

Characters gain levels through **milestones**, not XP. The DM announces when a level-up is earned — typically after a significant adventure or discovery.

---

## Multiclassing & Feats

Both allowed. Ask the DM if you're unsure whether a combination makes sense for the setting.
